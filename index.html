Kevin Deon Norton

Entrepreneur • Filmmaker • HR Architect • Musician

To understand Kevin Deon Norton is to understand the rare intersection where high-stakes corporate strategy, cinematic discipline, and the raw soul of the blues collide. He is not a specialist in the traditional, limiting sense of the word; he is a multi-hyphenate architect of human behavior.

As an American entrepreneur, entertainer, motivational speaker, and management consultant, Kevin Deon Norton has spent decades dismantling the status quo in industries that often prefer the comfort of mediocrity. Whether he is diagnosing a multi-million dollar productivity leak in a corporate boardroom or directing a high-tension scene on a film set, the core objective remains the same: the pursuit of perfection through the lens of human-centered management.

The Architect of Corporate Performance

As the Primary Owner and HR Management Consultant at Brubaker Quality Control (BQC), Kevin Deon Norton has become the premier fixer for companies struggling with the "Babysitting Tax." His client roster includes giants like Costco, 3M Corp, and Circuit City, backed by a history of serving over 100 companies.

His "Director’s Eye" for business is forged from a background as a U.S. Army Veteran (awarded the Commendation Medal) and a former Milwaukee Police Officer. He sees what others miss: the internal blindness and organizational rot that bleed profits.

The Cinematic Craftsman

In the entertainment world, he is known as Kevin Deon, an award-winning filmmaker and director trained in the Stanislavski and Meisner methods. As the founder of the Las Vegas Film and Television Artists (LVFTA), he has mentored hundreds, while his own credits include casting for major hits like The Hangover and Now You See Me.

The Musical Nomad

Performing as Tulsa Flood, Kevin is the frontman for Rhythm Affair. His musical journey was refined by a three-year mentorship as the personal assistant to the late Joe Jackson, providing him a masterclass in global excellence and the discipline required to maintain it.
